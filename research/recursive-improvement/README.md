# `RECURSIVE IMPROVEMENT`

> *The optimization loop.*

<br>

The point at which AI systems can meaningfully improve AI systems is the inflection point of all inflection points. It is the moment the curve decouples from human cognitive speed and begins to compound on its own terms.

We are not at that point. But we are closer than most people realize.

<br>

## Key observations

AI systems currently contribute to their own improvement in several ways: code generation for ML pipelines, hyperparameter optimization, architecture search, data curation and filtering, and increasingly, research ideation. Each of these contributions is partial. None constitutes a closed loop. But the trajectory from "AI as research tool" to "AI as research collaborator" to "AI as research driver" is visible and accelerating.

The critical threshold is **full-cycle automation**: a system that can identify a promising research direction, design an experiment, implement it, analyze the results, and apply the insights to improve itself — without human intervention. We are not there. We are also not as far away as the phrase "without human intervention" makes it sound.

<br>

## The open question

What happens to the rate of AI progress when the limiting factor is no longer human cognitive speed? If a system can run the research cycle in hours instead of months, and each cycle produces improvements that make the next cycle faster — what does that curve look like? We can do the math. The math is uncomfortable.
