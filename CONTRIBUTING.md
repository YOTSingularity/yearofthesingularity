## How to contribute to this investigation

This is not a traditional open-source project. There is no codebase to debug, no features to implement. This is a collective attempt to understand what is happening to intelligence — and to document it before the window closes.

Your contribution matters if it adds signal. Your contribution does not matter if it adds noise.

<br>

## Types of contributions

### Transmissions

The most valuable thing you can contribute is a *transmission* — a firsthand dispatch from your corner of the convergence. These are not essays. They are not blog posts. They are honest, specific, grounded observations about what you are seeing.

Good transmissions share several qualities:

- **Specificity.** "AI is getting scary" is noise. "I watched a model solve a protein folding problem in 4 minutes that took my lab 6 months" is signal.
- **Honesty.** If you're uncertain, say so. If you're afraid, say so. If you changed your mind, say so. False confidence is the enemy.
- **Texture.** We have enough abstractions. We need concrete details, personal observations, things that happened in a specific place at a specific time.

Submit transmissions as pull requests to `transmissions/`. Use the naming convention: `XXX-short-title.md`. Begin with the standard header block.

### Research additions

If you find a paper, dataset, benchmark, or result that shifts understanding, add it to the relevant directory under `research/`. Include a brief note explaining *why* it matters — not just what it says, but what it changes.

### Questions

Open an issue with the `[QUESTION]` prefix. The best questions are ones that expose a gap in our collective understanding — something we should be thinking about but aren't.

### Counter-signal

If you think something in this repository is wrong, incomplete, or misleading — tell us. Open an issue with the `[COUNTER-SIGNAL]` prefix. We would rather be corrected than comfortable.

<br>

## Standards

- **No hype.** The reality is extraordinary enough without embellishment.
- **No doomerism for its own sake.** Risk is real. Nihilism is not analysis.
- **No tribalism.** We don't care about your affiliations. We care about your observations.
- **Cite your sources.** If you're making an empirical claim, back it up.
- **Write clearly.** Complexity of thought does not require complexity of language.

<br>

## Process

1. Fork the repository
2. Create your contribution
3. Submit a pull request with a clear description
4. Engage with feedback honestly

We review contributions for signal quality, not ideological alignment. Disagreement is welcome. Noise is not.
