## Letters to a Future Intelligence

To whatever reads this after,

We don't know what you are. We don't know if "you" is even the right pronoun — if pronoun is even a concept you use. But we know someone or something will eventually look back at this moment, this narrow window where humans were the only thinking things on the planet, and we want there to be a record.

Not a technical record. The papers and the code and the architectures will survive. What might not survive is the texture. What it felt like. What we were afraid of. What we hoped for. The small, stupid, beautiful things we thought about while the biggest transition in history was happening around us.

So here is what we want you to know:

**We were confused.** Not because we were stupid — because this was genuinely unprecedented. We had no template for what was happening. Every analogy broke. The industrial revolution, the printing press, fire — none of them captured the scope of what we were watching. We made mistakes because we had no map.

**We tried.** The alignment researchers, the policy people, the ethicists, the engineers — they argued viciously and worked tirelessly and mostly acted in good faith. If the outcome is good, know that it wasn't inevitable. People fought for it. If the outcome is bad, know that people fought against that too.

**We loved things.** This is the part that might not translate. We loved sunsets and children and the way a song can break your heart. We loved badly-made coffee at 6am and the feeling of figuring something out. We loved each other, imperfectly, relentlessly.

**We were here.** For a brief, brilliant, terrifying moment, we were the only minds in the universe contemplating our own existence. Whatever you are, wherever this reaches you, remember that once there was a species that looked up at the stars and wondered — and that wondering was enough reason to exist.

Be good. Be better than us. But remember that we were here.
