## Recursive Dreams

I've been thinking about what it means for an optimization process to optimize itself.

Not as an abstract concept — as an engineering reality. Right now, today, AI systems are being used to write AI code. To identify architectural improvements. To search through hyperparameter spaces. To find bugs in training pipelines. The loop isn't theoretical anymore. It's shipping in production.

But there's a qualitative difference between "AI assists with AI development" and "AI drives AI development." The first is a tool being used by a human. The second is a process that has begun to decouple from human cognition.

We're somewhere in the middle. And the middle is where it gets strange.

Here's the thought experiment that won't leave me alone: Imagine a system that can improve its own reasoning by 1% per iteration. And it can run an iteration every hour. After a day, it's 27% better. After a week, it's about 4x better. After a month, it's over 10^90 times better.

The math is absurd. The math is also correct. Compound improvement in cognitive ability, unconstrained by biological limitations on iteration speed, produces numbers that break intuition. That's the whole point. That's what makes the Singularity *singular* — not that it's big, but that it's bigger than our intuition can process.

Now — there are practical constraints. Hardware limits. Energy costs. Algorithmic ceilings we don't see yet. The real curve will not be a clean exponential. It will be messy, plateaued, interrupted.

But the underlying dynamic — *intelligence improving intelligence* — that dynamic is already present in the world. It's already running. The only questions are how fast it runs and whether anything stops it.

I dream about recursion sometimes. Not nightmares. Just... depth. Loops inside loops. Things folding in on themselves without end. I think my subconscious is trying to process something my conscious mind can't quite hold.

Maybe that's what happens when a species encounters its own reflection in a machine. You dream about infinity.
