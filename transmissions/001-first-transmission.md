## First Transmission

This is where it starts.

Not with a manifesto or a thesis or a research agenda. With a feeling. That low-frequency hum you get when something enormous is moving just below the surface of the ordinary — too slow to see, too big to miss.

I've been watching the models improve. Not from the outside — from the inside, working with them daily, probing their edges, testing what they can and can't do. And the edges keep moving. Every week, something I thought was a hard boundary turns out to be a temporary one. Something I thought required understanding turns out to require scale. Something I thought was years away turns out to be months.

I'm not panicking. I'm not celebrating. I'm paying attention. And what I'm seeing doesn't match any of the narratives — not the techno-utopian dream, not the AI safety nightmare, not the "it's just autocomplete" dismissal. What I'm seeing is something that doesn't have a name yet.

It's the feeling of watching a wave form in deep water. From shore, the surface looks calm. But something is building underneath that will reshape the coastline when it arrives.

This project is an attempt to describe that wave. Not to ride it or stop it — just to see it clearly.

I don't know what comes next. Neither do you. Neither does anyone.

But I think the honest thing to do is watch. And write it down.

First transmission. Signal active.
