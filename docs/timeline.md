## A chronology of the convergence

This timeline tracks the key inflection points in the approach toward artificial general intelligence and beyond. It is necessarily incomplete. It is updated as our understanding changes. Dates are approximate where exact dates feel misleadingly precise.

<br>

---

### `THE FOUNDATIONS` — 2012-2019

**2012** — AlexNet wins ImageNet by a massive margin, demonstrating that deep learning at scale works. The neural network winter ends.

**2014** — GANs introduced. Machines begin generating images. The concept of "artificial creativity" shifts from philosophy to engineering.

**2016** — AlphaGo defeats Lee Sedol. A machine masters a game long thought to require human intuition. The cultural shock is felt globally.

**2017** — The Transformer architecture is published. "Attention Is All You Need" becomes the most consequential machine learning paper of the decade. The foundation for everything that follows.

**2018** — BERT and GPT demonstrate that pre-training on large text corpora produces surprisingly capable language models. The scaling hypothesis begins to form.

**2019** — GPT-2 is deemed "too dangerous to release." In retrospect, this moment marks the first time an AI lab confronted the implications of its own work. It would not be the last.

<br>

### `THE SCALING ERA` — 2020-2023

**2020** — GPT-3 arrives with 175 billion parameters. Few-shot learning emerges — models doing tasks they were never explicitly trained for. Scaling laws are empirically confirmed: more compute, more data, more parameters = more capability, predictably.

**2021** — Codex writes functional code. DALL-E generates images from text descriptions. The boundary between "language model" and "general reasoning system" begins to blur. Models demonstrate emergent abilities at sufficient scale.

**2022** — ChatGPT reaches 100 million users faster than any technology in history. The general public encounters AI capability for the first time. The Overton window shifts overnight. InstructGPT demonstrates that human feedback can be used to align model behavior.

**2023** — GPT-4 matches or exceeds human performance on bar exams, medical licensing exams, and standardized tests. Multimodal models process text, images, and code simultaneously. The agent paradigm begins — models that don't just respond but *act.*

<br>

### `THE ACCELERATION` — 2024-2025

**2024** — Reasoning models emerge. Chain-of-thought at inference time. Models begin to "think" through problems step by step, visibly. Benchmark saturation accelerates — tests designed to last years are cleared in months. AI-assisted AI research becomes standard practice in major labs.

**2025** — Autonomous agent architectures mature. Models operate independently across multi-step tasks with decreasing human oversight. The recursive loop tightens. AI systems contribute meaningfully to their own improvement. The research cycle compresses.

<br>

### `THE CONVERGENCE` — 2026

**2026** — You are here.

Multiple exponential curves are approaching vertical simultaneously. Research timelines that were measured in years are now measured in weeks. The gap between "state of the art" and "surpassed" is narrowing toward days. Public discourse cannot keep pace with developments. The question shifts from *"Will we reach AGI?"* to *"When did we reach it?"*

The timeline becomes harder to write from this point forward. Not because events stop happening — but because they happen faster than documentation can track.
